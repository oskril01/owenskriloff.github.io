Loss-Landscape Geometry of Neural Networks

Write-Up Code

This project investigates how neural network depth affects generalization by analyzing the geometry of the training-loss landscape. Fully connected networks of varying depths were trained on MNIST and analyzed using curvature and loss metrics at their converged minima. Two summary statistics—a parameter cluster radius and a depth-dependent trade-off weight—revealed an optimal window near depth 4. Adding a global curvature penalty reduced validation error by over 15%, showing that Hessian-based regularization can improve generalization in a principled, architecture-aware way.

Entropy and Energy in Reaction-Diffusion PDEs

Write-Up Slides Code

This project explores how two complementary metrics—Shannon entropy and the energy norm—can be used to characterize the behavior of reaction-diffusion systems. By solving a coupled system across a range of diffusivity and reactivity parameters, we generated solutions exhibiting diverse dynamic regimes. Entropy proved effective at identifying sharp transitions with minimal time data, while the energy norm captured fine-grained variations within evolving patterns. Together, these measures offer a powerful framework for understanding phase transitions and emergent behavior in spatially distributed systems.

Industrial Scale Amikacin Biochemical Plant Design

Superpro PFD

David Gillingham, Ivy Le, Youngseo Yi, and I collaborated on a Class IV economic report for a biochemical plant designed to produce 450 kg of amikacin per year. Amikacin is an antibiotic used to treat drug-resistant infections and is synthesized by chemically modifying kanamycin, another antibiotic. We designed a chemical process based on state-of-the-art research and modeled it using SuperPro Designer. The proposed facility includes three integrated sections: a bioprocess plant for kanamycin production via fermentation, a chemical synthesis unit to prepare the side group, and a final unit to couple the side group with kanamycin to yield amikacin.
